This My Vocabulary Webpage For Week 14

Wireframe

An Image Or Set Of Images Which Displays The Functional Elements Of A Website Or Page Typically Used For Planning A Sites Structure And Functionality

Sitemaps Visual

A Diagrammatic Representation Of A Websites Structure Showing How Pages Are Organized And Connected Acting As A Blueprint For Navigation And Content Planning

Website Content Categories And Taxonomy

A Classification System Used To Categorize Different Types Of Content On Your Business Site

User Story

A Short Informal And Natural Language Description Of A Feature Or Functionality From The Perspective Of The End User Used To Capture Requirements And Prioritize Work

Mood Board

This is Used To Visually Show The Feelings Or Values That The Digital Product Should Create

Wurman's LATCH

A Mnemonic Device (Location, Alphabet, Time, Category, Hierarchy) That Helps Organize Information By Identifying Five Fundamental Ways To Structure And Categorize Data Making It More Accessible And Understandable

Sans-Serif Fonts - Serif Fonts

Serif Fonts Have Those Decorative Lines Or Tapers (Also Commonly Referred To As “Tails” Or “Feet”) While Sans Serif Fonts Don't

Usability

A Method Of Evaluating A Products Ease Of Use By Observing Real Users As They Attempt To Complete Tasks Aiming To Identify Areas For Improvement And Enhance The Overall User Experience

Sitemaps for Google (XML)

It's An Guide To Google And Other Engines Of The Pages You Want Them To Crawl And Index

Story Board

A Visual Representation Of A Planned Sequence Of Events

Static And Variable Web Fonts

Variable Fonts Maintain A Single File Having All The Possible Variations & Static Fonts Do Not Support Any CSS Transition Or Animation

Card Sort

A Method Where participants group items (like website pages or concepts) on cards into categories that make sense to them, revealing how users understand and organize information
